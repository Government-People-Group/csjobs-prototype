(1) Vacancy is set up
Event Who Status Testing notes
Vacancy holder starts the process of creating a new vacancy
Notes
They provide a few simple details at the start. The proposed job title and the grade, the profession that the role will be aligned to and finally their department. This information is assumed to be readily available to vacancy holders, as it is required during the business case and approval step.

The approval process is department-specific, but this common, basic information should be attainable.

We use this information throughout the service, and certain features are dependent on them (e.g., the job library and templates).

VACANCY HOLDER TESTED Tested multiple times. Not certain of what all the different options will be in the taxonomies used under the various components but the page works well and users are able to make a decision.
Some vacancy holders create job vacancies written in both Welsh and English
Notes
Vacancy holders come from organisations that span the Four Nations. Welsh-speaking vacancy holders and candidates alike, expect to have option to find and apply for jobs using their native language. Whilst not depicted in the prototype, it's important that the vacancy set-up service supports this need.

VACANCY HOLDER N / A Welsh-language support in campaign set-up is a must-have requirement.
Offered a choice of 3 vacancy set-up options:

  1. Use a Civil Service template
  2. Make a copy of a previous job advert
  3. Create a new vacancy
 VACANCY HOLDER TESTED Tested multiple times. Not certain of what all the different options will be but the page works well and users are able to make a decision.
A. Use a Civil Service template
Notes
Sumayyah is an infrequent vacancy holder, relying on the guidance of colleagues, recommendation provided by the service and using standard templates to set-up a vacancy. Templates that offer a pre-defined assessment ‘pack’ (selection criteria and assessment methods carefully chosen by experts) might help overcome issues felt around Success Profiles.


 VACANCY HOLDER TESTED The template selection page is a new version and untested.
B. Make a copy of a previous job advert
Notes
Amisha is a frequent vacancy holder and experienced recruiter (one which others in her team look to for guidance). She has run recruitment campaigns in the past and usually makes a copy of what she feels is the best match from which to set-up her new vacancy.

  • With the exception of dates and times most of the content is reproduced in the new vacancy task-list, AS-IS.
  • It's unlikely that there will be many constraints on the kind of vacancy that can be copied, the vacancy holder is prompted to review and edit the new vacancy set-up as required.
  • Apart from the feature of copying a previous vacancy to pre-fill a new vacancy - there aren't any tools or mechanisms to allow individuals, departments and professions to create a new template.

 VACANCY HOLDER UNTESTED The pages that support the advert copy feature are untested. The effect of copying an advert, in terms of duplicated content has been assumed to be fairly comprehensive with the exception of dates and content derived from workflow.
C. Create a new vacancy
Notes
The vacancy holder is presented with a blank task-list to set-up their vacancy.

The vacancy holder's department may institute policy-led defaults on some of the options available (e.g., the level of vetting required at the pre-employment checks stage) or provide pre-filled boilerplate copy for some content.

VACANCY HOLDER TESTED The transition from the selection screen before to the task list as a starting point for campaign set-up has been tested many times successfully.

The structure is likely to change further as aspects of the proposition and experience are further refined.
1. About the vacancy

2. Contract information

If they decide to pursue the reserve list option their current journey of campaign set-up is suspended and new branch (the reserve list) is followed (they have the option of cancelling and reverting back to their set-up).

VACANCY HOLDER TESTED The campaign dates page is based on an earlier version which has been tested.
C. Submit for approval
Notes
Upon submission of the new campaign, and before the job advert is published to production, the vacancy must be approved. This workflow places a gate checkpoint to assess quality and conformance with the Civil Service Commission principles. Whilst not depicted in prototype, an approval workflow is triggered at this point and should be supported.

VACANCY HOLDER UNTESTED The final submission (for approval) and confirmation screens are new and untested. The content on these pages are likely to change depending on the workflow and governance to be afforded by the TOBE service.

(5) Interviews are set up and arranged
Event Who Status Testing notes
(a) interview set up screens
Event Who Status Testing notes
Schedule of interview slots created based on chair and panel's availability
Notes
